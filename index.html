ABOUT THIS ALBUM

Flower Boy (promoted as Scum Fuck Flower Boy)
is Tyler’s course-correction, surprisingly meditative and beautifully colored,
a collage of memories and daydreams that trades bratty subversion for
reflection and self-improvement. He probes the things that shaped his psyche—loneliness, isolation,
and disorientation—and focuses on outgrowing friendships,
balancing the pull of nostalgia and the necessity for growth.
Not only is Flower Boy Tyler’s most trenchant work, it’s his most
inclusive: “Find Your Wings”: The Album, gentle and liberating.
“Tell these black kids they can be who they are,” he raps on
“Where This Flower Blooms,” as he grows into the artist he’s always longed to be,
and perhaps always was.

“Дом с нормальными явлениями“ — это
алкогольный угар и путеводитель по жизни Павлодара. Города,
который кажется мрачным, агрессивным, напористым. Где, кажется,
на завтрак проще достать бутылку водки, чем пакетик “липтона”.
Сука тащит на дно, вокруг — притоны и трэп-хаты,
в герое — поллитра водки в доле с марихуаной, а проблемы в
чувствах лечит только план. Если описывать альбом одним словом,
то лучше всего подойдёт "энергия".

Playboi Carti is an exercise in
efficiency—more beats, less words in number and variation.
It’s an exaggerated take on an old formula that all but
guarantees his tracks will become earworms, which they do.
Verses and hooks smash into each other with repetition,
as his signature ad-libs command space in a way not even
Jim Jones or Young Jeezy could’ve imagined. Here, they aren’t
just highlights or devices to advance the song’s conversation;
they’re the main attraction. Ad-libs become parts of hooks,
outlines of lyrics and, sometimes, simply just are the lyrics.
